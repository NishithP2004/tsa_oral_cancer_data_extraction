This patient is a 61-year-old female who underwent surgery on October 24, 2013, for a moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma located on the tongue. The initial surgical procedure involved a selective neck dissection, ultimately revealing lymph node involvement classified as N2c according to the 8th Edition Clinical Tumor Staging System. The tumor was classified as T2. Following surgery, adjuvant therapy was initiated. A subsequent follow-up on November 1, 2017, indicated no evidence of disease. The case represents a moderately aggressive presentation of oral squamous cell carcinoma with initial nodal involvement requiring a comprehensive surgical approach.

The patient is a 65-year-old male who underwent surgery on March 6th, 2014, for a diagnosis of moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma located on the tongue. The tumor was classified as T3, indicating extension beyond the margins of the tongue, and N0, signifying no detectable lymph node involvement. A selective neck dissection was performed. Pathology revealed a moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma. Following surgery, no adjuvant therapy was administered. The final follow-up, dated May 8th, 2014, reported no evidence of disease.

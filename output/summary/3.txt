This patient is a 51-year-old female who underwent a radical neck dissection and surgery for moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the tongue, diagnosed on 9/24/2019. The tumor was classified as T3, indicating an invasive tumor with significant local extension, and staged as N3b, signifying the involvement of multiple levels of lymph nodes in the neck. Pathology confirmed a moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma. Following surgery, adjuvant therapy was initiated. At the last follow-up on 2/19/2020, there was no evidence of disease. This represents a successful outcome with complete resection and no recurrence detected.

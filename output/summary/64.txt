This 59-year-old male patient was diagnosed with a poorly differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the tongue, first identified on August 31, 2017. Surgical intervention involved a selective neck dissection, alongside confirmation of N2c nodal involvement according to the 8th Edition staging system. Pathology confirmed the tumor was a T3, indicating substantial local invasion. Following surgery, adjuvant therapy was administered. The last documented follow-up on December 6, 2019, reported no evidence of disease. The nodal status was confirmed as N1. Despite the initial concerning T3 and N2c stage, the patient's response to treatment and subsequent surveillance resulted in complete remission at the last follow-up.

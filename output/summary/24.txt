This 43-year-old female patient presented with a moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the tongue, diagnosed on 3/12/2020. The tumor was staged clinically as T2, indicating a local extent of invasion, and pathologically confirmed as T2. Critically, the pathological examination revealed no evidence of nodal involvement (N0) and was staged accordingly. The patient underwent a selective neck dissection. Following the initial surgery, there has been no evidence of disease recurrence at the last follow-up on 8/26/2022. The overall clinical picture suggests successful surgical management and an excellent long-term prognosis.

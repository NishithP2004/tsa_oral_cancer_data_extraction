This patient is a 61-year-old female who presented with a moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the tongue, diagnosed on October 24, 2013. The initial tumor was classified as T2, indicating a larger tumor size, and N2c, signifying the involvement of the neck lymph nodes. A selective neck dissection was performed at the time of surgery. Pathological examination revealed the carcinoma was moderately differentiated. Following surgery, adjuvant therapy was initiated. Subsequent follow-up on November 1, 2017, revealed no evidence of disease. The final clinical tumor staging, according to the 8th edition, remains T1, suggesting a smaller residual tumor at the time of last assessment.

This patient is a 46-year-old male who presented with a well-differentiated squamous cell carcinoma originating in the alveolus of the lower lip. The tumor was initially diagnosed on October 3rd, 2017. Due to the extent of the disease, a selective neck dissection was performed, revealing nodal involvement (N1). Subsequent pathological examination confirmed a T4a staging, indicating a large primary tumor. Further pathological analysis revealed nodal involvement classified as N2b. Following initial surgery and adjuvant therapy (details not specified), the patient was last followed on December 19th, 2019, and at that time, there was no evidence of disease recurrence. The tumor was initially staged according to the 8th edition as T4a and N1.

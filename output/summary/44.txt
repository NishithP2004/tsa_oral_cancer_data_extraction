The patient is a 47-year-old male who presented with a Moderately Differentiated Squamous Cell Carcinoma of the tongue, diagnosed on 2/22/2021. The initial surgical intervention involved a selective neck dissection (levels 1-4) performed on the same date. Pathological examination revealed a tumor classified as T4a, indicating an invasive lesion with extension into deeper structures, and confirmed N3b nodal involvement, indicating metastasis to regional lymph nodes. Subsequently, the patient underwent adjuvant therapy (details unspecified). At the last follow-up on 7/31/2021, there was no evidence of disease, despite the aggressive initial staging. The location of the primary tumor was on the tongue.

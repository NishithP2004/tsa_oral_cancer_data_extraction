This 62-year-old male patient presented with a well-differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the buccal mucosa. The tumor was surgically resected on August 6, 2019, with a subsequent selective neck dissection performed, revealing no involved lymph nodes (N0). Pathological staging according to the 8th edition classified the tumor as T4a, indicating a large primary tumor. Following surgery, there was no evidence of disease at the last follow-up conducted on June 13, 2020. This represents a successful outcome, with complete resection and no nodal involvement.

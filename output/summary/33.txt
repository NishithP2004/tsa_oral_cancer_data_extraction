This patient is a 66-year-old male who underwent surgery on March 6th, 2014, for a diagnosis of moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the tongue. The tumor was classified as T3, indicating extension beyond the confines of the tongue, and N0, signifying no detectable lymph node involvement. A selective neck dissection was performed at the time of initial surgery. Following the initial resection, the patient received no adjuvant therapy. The last follow-up examination on July 13th, 2021, revealed no evidence of disease recurrence.

This patient is a 60-year-old male who presented with a moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the tongue, diagnosed on January 19, 2017. The tumor was classified as T4a and N2c, indicating a large primary tumor and significant nodal involvement. A radical neck dissection was performed as the initial surgical treatment. Following surgery, adjuvant therapy was administered. Unfortunately, the patient experienced a local recurrence of the cancer. Subsequently, further nodal recurrence was observed. Despite treatment, the patient ultimately succumbed to the disease, with the final follow-up occurring on November 9, 2017.

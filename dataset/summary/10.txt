This patient is a 25-year-old female presenting with a history of moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the tongue, diagnosed on 12/28/2015. The initial tumor was classified as T3, indicating a larger primary tumor. Following surgical intervention – a modified radical neck dissection – the patient underwent adjuvant therapy. Critically, the patient experienced local recurrence, initially identified on 10/17/2021. Subsequent investigations revealed nodal recurrence as well, classified as N0 according to the 8th edition. The tumor was classified as T4a at the time of initial diagnosis, indicating extensive involvement. Despite the aggressive nature of the initial diagnosis and subsequent recurrence, the patient’s last follow-up at 10/17/2021 revealed no evidence of disease. The surgery was performed ipsilaterally, and pathological staging was N0.

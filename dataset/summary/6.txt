This patient is a 58-year-old male who presented with a moderately differentiated squamous cell carcinoma of the floor of mouth, diagnosed on March 24, 2017. The initial surgical intervention involved a modified radical neck dissection. Subsequent adjuvant therapy was initiated. Following this, the patient experienced local recurrence of the tumor on February 15, 2021. The pathological assessment at the time of recurrence revealed a T4a primary tumor and an N3b nodal stage, indicating extensive local disease and significant nodal involvement. Despite this, the patient remains alive and under follow-up as of February 15, 2021. The clinical picture reflects a complex and aggressive form of oral cancer with a challenging treatment course.
